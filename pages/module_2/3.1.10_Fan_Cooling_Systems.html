3.1.10 Fan Cooling Systems

Why Cooling Matters

Computer components emit heat due to electrical resistance as current passes through them. Without cooling, rising temperatures can cause malfunctions or permanent damage. All CPUs require cooling to maintain safe operating temperatures, as do memory modules and graphics adapters.

Components and Their Cooling Solutions

Component Cooling Solution Notes
CPU Heatsink + fan (HSF) or liquid cooler Most critical component to cool; sits directly on top of the CPU with thermal paste in between
GPU (Graphics Card) Onboard fan(s) and heatsink High-end GPUs may have two or three fans; some use liquid cooling
RAM (Memory Modules) Passive heatspreaders or small fans High-speed or overclocked RAM may include metal heatspreaders to dissipate heat
Case (Chassis) Case fans (intake and exhaust) Move air through the entire case to cool all components; placement determines airflow direction
PSU Built-in fan Cools the PSU internally and also helps exhaust warm air from the case

Fan Placement and Airflow

Proper airflow direction is essential for effective cooling. Fans are configured as either intake (pulling cool air in) or exhaust (pushing warm air out).

  • Intake fans – Typically placed at the front or bottom of the case; draw cool air in
  • Exhaust fans – Typically placed at the rear or top of the case; expel warm air out
  • A good airflow setup creates a front-to-back, bottom-to-top flow of air through the case
  • Poor airflow leads to hot spots and reduced component lifespan

Thermal Paste

Thermal paste (also called thermal compound or thermal interface material) is applied between the CPU and its heatsink. It fills microscopic gaps to improve heat transfer from the CPU to the heatsink. Without it, air pockets form and reduce cooling efficiency.

Key Terms

  • Heatsink – A metal component that absorbs and disperses heat away from a component
  • HSF (Heatsink + Fan) – A combined cooling unit that sits on top of the CPU
  • Thermal paste – Compound applied between CPU and heatsink to improve heat transfer
  • Intake fan – A fan that pulls cool air into the case
  • Exhaust fan – A fan that pushes warm air out of the case
  • Airflow – The directed movement of air through the case to cool components
  • Thermal efficiency – How effectively a component manages and reduces heat output
