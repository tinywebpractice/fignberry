3.1.11 Heat Sinks and Thermal Paste

Types of Heat Sinks

Type Also Known As Has Fan? Used By How It Cools
Passive Heat spreader No Memory modules (RAM) Increased surface area and passive air movement
Active HSF (Heatsink + Fan) Yes CPUs, high-end GPUs, some motherboard chipsets Copper or aluminum fins + fan creates forced air convection

Thermal Paste vs Thermal Pad

Type Application Reliability Notes
Thermal paste More precise — applied manually More reliable Fills microscopic air gaps between CPU and heatsink for efficient heat transfer
Thermal pad Easier to apply Less reliable Softens when heated; convenient but not as effective as paste

Note: Both thermal paste and thermal pads serve the same purpose — eliminating air gaps between the CPU and heatsink to ensure efficient heat transfer.

CPU Heat Sink Assembly (Top to Bottom)

  • CPU Fan – Mounted on top of the heatsink; creates forced air convection
  • CPU Heatsink – Copper or aluminum block with fins; absorbs and disperses heat from the CPU
  • Thermal paste / pad – Applied between heatsink and CPU to eliminate air gaps
  • CPU – The processor generating the heat
  • CPU Socket – On the motherboard; holds the CPU in place

Mounting the Heat Sink

CPU heat sinks are clamped to the motherboard using one of two mechanisms:

  • Retaining clips – Clip around the edges of the heatsink to hold it in place
  • Push pins – Press into the motherboard and can be released and reset with a half-turn of a screwdriver

Key Terms

  • Passive heat sink – A heat sink with no fan; relies on surface area and natural airflow (e.g. RAM heat spreaders)
  • Active heat sink – A heat sink with an attached fan that creates forced air convection
  • Heat spreader – Another name for a passive heat sink, commonly used on RAM
  • Thermal paste – Compound applied between CPU and heatsink to improve heat transfer
  • Thermal pad – Pre-applied alternative to thermal paste; easier to use but less reliable
  • Forced air convection – Cooling method that uses a fan to actively move air over a heatsink
  • Push pins – Heatsink mounting mechanism released with a half-turn of a screwdriver
