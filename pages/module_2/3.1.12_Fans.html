3.1.12 Fans

Heat Sinks vs Fans

A heat sink is a passive cooling device — it requires no electricity and relies on good airflow to work effectively. When a PC generates more heat than passive cooling can handle, fans are needed to actively improve airflow and dissipate heat.

Tip: To help passive cooling work better, minimize cable clutter inside the case and cover spare adapter slots with blanking plates to prevent warm air from recirculating.

Where Fans Are Used

Location Role Airflow Direction
Front chassis vents Intake Draws cool air into the case
Rear chassis exhaust Exhaust Expels warm air out of the case
Power supply (PSU) Exhaust Cools the PSU and helps remove warm air
CPU heat sink Active cooling Forces air over heatsink fins; connects to motherboard fan power port

Fan Speed and Monitoring

  • Thermometer sensors at each fan location monitor temperature and set appropriate fan speeds
  • Sensors also detect fan failures and can alert the system if a fan stops working
  • CPU fans must be connected to a motherboard fan power port for speed control and monitoring

Airflow Management

  • Shrouds and baffles – Plastic channels that direct airflow specifically over the CPU; attached with plastic clips
  • Blanking plates – Cover unused expansion slots to prevent warm air from recirculating inside the case
  • Cable management – Keeping cables tidy reduces obstruction to airflow

Maintenance

Both fans and heat sinks become less effective when dust accumulates. Clean them periodically using:

  • Soft brush – Gently removes dust from fins and fan blades
  • Compressed air – Blows dust out of hard-to-reach areas
  • PC-approved vacuum cleaner – Safely removes dust without generating harmful static

Key Terms

  • Passive cooling – Cooling without electricity; relies on surface area and natural airflow (e.g. a bare heat sink)
  • Active cooling – Cooling that uses a fan or pump to move air or liquid
  • Blanking plate – A cover for unused expansion slots that prevents warm air recirculation
  • Shroud / baffle – A plastic channel that directs airflow over a specific component such as the CPU
  • Thermometer sensor – Monitors temperature at fan locations and adjusts fan speed accordingly
  • Fan power port – Motherboard connector that supplies power and enables speed control for fans
