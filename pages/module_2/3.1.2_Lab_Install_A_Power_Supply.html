Lab Scenario

A computer in the IT Administration Office has a failed power supply. The defective unit has already been tested, confirmed bad, and removed from the system. Your task is to choose a compatible replacement PSU, install it in the case, connect the required power leads, and start the computer successfully.

Step 1 - Locate the Correct Power Supply

  • Switch to Motherboard view and select the motherboard.
  • Open Item Detail to confirm the required connectors. The motherboard needs a 24-pin connection and an 8-pin CPU connection.
  • Under Inventory > Power Supplies, review the available units and choose the one that includes those connectors along with SATA power and graphics card support.

Step 2 - Install the Power Supply

  • Drag the correct PSU into the space below the motherboard in the case.
  • Connect the 20+4 pin connector to the motherboard's main power port on the right edge, upper half of the board.
  • Connect the 8-pin CPU connector to the CPU power port on the top edge, left side of the motherboard.

Step 3 - Connect the SATA Hard Drives

  • Attach a SATA 15-pin power connector to the power port on the first hard drive.
  • Attach another SATA 15-pin power connector to the second hard drive.
  • Attach a third SATA 15-pin power connector to the third hard drive.

Note: Each hard drive needs its own SATA power connection.

Step 4 - Plug In and Power On

  • Switch to Back view of the computer.
  • Select the top power cord from the power strip.
  • Drag the AC female connector to the PSU power port on the back of the system.
  • Flip the PSU power switch to the On position.
  • Switch to Front view and press the power button to start the computer.

Connector Summary

Connector Destination
20+4 pin Motherboard main power port (right edge, upper half)
8-pin CPU CPU power port (top edge, left side)
SATA 15-pin (x3) One connector per SATA hard drive
AC female connector PSU AC input on the back of the computer

Key Terms

  • 20+4 pin connector - Main ATX motherboard power connector
  • 8-pin CPU connector - Dedicated power connector for the processor
  • SATA 15-pin power connector - Power connector used for SATA storage devices
  • AC female connector - Connection point for the power cord from the wall outlet or power strip
  • PSU power switch - Rear power switch that must be turned on before the computer can boot

Important: Never open the PSU housing itself. Internal capacitors can retain dangerous voltage even after the unit has been unplugged.
