3.1.3 Wattage Rating

What is Wattage Rating?

Power is the rate at which energy is generated or used, measured in watts (W), calculated as Voltage x Current (V x I). A PSU's output capability is measured in watts - this is its wattage rating.

Typical PSU Wattage by System Type

System Type Typical PSU Wattage
Standard Desktop 400-500 W
Gaming PC 600 W or more
Enterprise Workstation / Server 300 W+, sometimes exceeding 1000 W

Risks of an Underpowered PSU

  • System Instability - Insufficient power can cause random shutdowns, reboots, or crashes
  • Component Damage - Running at or beyond capacity causes overheating, potentially damaging the PSU or other components

Note: CPU power requirements alone can range from 17 W to over 100 W. Use an online calculator such as Cooler Master's power supply calculator to estimate your system's needs.

Output Rails & Power Distribution

A rail is a wire providing current at a specific voltage. When selecting a PSU for high-power systems, check how power is distributed across each rail.

Output Rail (VDC) Maximum Load (A) Maximum Output (W)
+3.3 20 130
+5 20 130
+12 33 396
-12 0.8 9.6
+5 (standby) 2.5 12.5

Note: The +3.3 V and +5 V outputs have a combined limit. The +12 VDC rail is the most important for modern computers due to heavy usage by CPUs and GPUs.

Energy Efficiency

A PSU does not convert all AC power perfectly into DC. The unused power is lost as heat. Example: a 300 W PSU at 75% efficiency draws 400 W from the outlet - 100 W is wasted as heat.

PSUs are rated under the 80 PLUS certification program:

Certification 20% Load 50% Load 100% Load
80 PLUS Bronze 82% 85% 82%
80 PLUS Silver 85% 88% 85%
80 PLUS Gold 87% 90% 87%
80 PLUS Platinum 90% 92% 89%
80 PLUS Titanium 92% (90% at 10%) 94% 90%

ENERGY STAR 80 PLUS compliant PSUs must be at least 80% efficient at 20-100% load. Higher efficiency ratings mean less wasted energy, less heat, quieter operation, and longer component lifespan.

Key Terms

  • Wattage Rating - The maximum power output a PSU can deliver (measured in watts)
  • Rail - A wire providing current at a specific voltage (e.g. +12 VDC rail)
  • 80 PLUS - Certification program rating PSU energy efficiency
  • Efficiency - The percentage of AC power successfully converted to usable DC power
  • +12 VDC Rail - The most critical rail in modern PCs; powers CPUs and GPUs
