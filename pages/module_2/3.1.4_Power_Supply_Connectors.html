3.1.4 Power Supply Connectors

Overview

Each PSU has multiple power connectors that supply DC voltage to the motherboard and devices at 3.3 VDC, 5 VDC, and 12 VDC. Voltage regulators adjust the supplied voltage to match each component's requirements.

Common PSU Connectors

Connector Also Known As Powers
P1 Connector 24-pin ATX power connector Motherboard (main power)
Molex Connector — Older drives and devices
SATA Power Connector — SATA drives (HDDs, SSDs, optical drives)
4/8-pin Connector EPS / CPU power connector CPU power port on motherboard
6/8/16-pin Connector PCIe power connector PCIe adapter cards (GPUs)

Key Terms

  • P1 Connector - The main 24-pin ATX connector supplying power to the motherboard
  • Molex Connector - Older 4-pin connector used for legacy drives and devices
  • SATA Power Connector - Powers SATA hard drives, SSDs, and optical drives
  • PCIe Connector - 6, 8, or 16-pin connector supplying power to graphics cards
  • Voltage Regulator - Adjusts the supplied voltage to match a component's specific requirements
