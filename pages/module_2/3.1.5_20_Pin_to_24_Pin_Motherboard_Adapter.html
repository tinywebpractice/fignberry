3.1.5 20-pin to 24-pin Motherboard Adapter

Overview

The ATX PSU standard has undergone several revisions, each specifying different connector form factors. The P1 connector connects the PSU to the motherboard and has evolved from 20-pin to 24-pin over time.

P1 Connector Versions

Version Pin Layout Usage
Original ATX 20-pin (2x10) Older motherboards
Modern ATX 24-pin (2x12) Most current systems
Adapter Cable 20+4-pin Compatibility with older 20-pin motherboards

Wire Color Codes (Original ATX 20-pin)

Wire Color Voltage / Function
Black Ground
Yellow +12 V
Red +5 V
Orange +3.3 V

Key Terms

  • P1 Connector - The main power connector between the PSU and motherboard
  • 20-pin (2x10) - Original ATX P1 connector format
  • 24-pin (2x12) - Modern ATX P1 connector format used by most current systems
  • 20+4-pin Adapter - Allows a modern 24-pin PSU to connect to an older 20-pin motherboard
