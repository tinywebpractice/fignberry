3.1.6 Modular Power Supplies

What is a Modular Power Supply?

A modular power supply has detachable power connector cables, allowing you to connect only the cables your system actually needs. This is in contrast to a non-modular PSU, where all cables are permanently attached regardless of whether they are used.

Modular vs Non-Modular PSU

Feature Non-Modular PSU Modular PSU
Cables All permanently attached Detachable — connect only what you need
Cable clutter More unused cables inside chassis Minimal — unused cables removed
Airflow & cooling Can be restricted by excess cables Improved due to reduced clutter

Benefits of a Modular PSU

  • Reduced cable clutter – Only necessary cables are connected inside the chassis
  • Improved airflow – Fewer cables means better air circulation and cooling
  • Easier cable management – Cleaner builds and simpler upgrades

Example: A non-modular PSU may include four or five Molex or SATA connectors, but if the PC only needs two, the unused cables still take up space. A modular PSU lets you remove them entirely.

Key Terms

  • Modular PSU – A power supply with detachable cables, allowing only the needed connectors to be used
  • Non-Modular PSU – A power supply where all cables are permanently attached
  • Chassis – The PC case that houses all components including the PSU
  • Airflow – The movement of air through the case; improved when cable clutter is reduced
