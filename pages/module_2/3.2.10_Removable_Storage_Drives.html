3.2.10 Removable Storage Drives

What is Removable Storage?

Removable storage refers to devices that can be moved between computers without opening the case, or to media that can be removed from its drive.

Drive Enclosures

HDDs and SSDs can be made portable by placing them inside an enclosure. The enclosure provides:

  • A data interface – USB, Thunderbolt, or eSATA
  • A power connector – if required by the drive
  • Physical protection for the disk

Note: Some enclosures are NAS (Network Attached Storage) devices that connect directly to a network. Advanced enclosures can host multiple disks configured as a RAID array.

Flash Drives

Also known as a USB drive, thumb drive, or pen drive, a flash drive consists of a flash memory board with a USB connector and a protective cover. It plugs into any available USB port.

Memory Cards

Commonly used in digital cameras, smartphones, and tablets. A card reader is required to use a memory card with a PC. Readers typically fit in a front-facing drive bay and connect to the motherboard via a USB controller or USB header. A reader may also come as an expansion card.

Note: Smaller form factor cards can be used with standard-size readers via an adapter (caddy). Most multi-card readers support several card types simultaneously.

Key Terms

  • Removable storage – Storage that can be moved between computers without opening the case
  • Drive enclosure – A case that makes an internal HDD or SSD portable by adding a data interface and physical protection
  • NAS (Network Attached Storage) – An enclosure connected directly to a network for shared access
  • Flash drive – A portable flash memory device with a USB connector; also called a USB drive, thumb drive, or pen drive
  • Memory card – Removable flash storage used in cameras, phones, and tablets; requires a card reader for PC use
  • Card reader – A device that reads memory cards; connects to the motherboard via USB controller or header
  • SD / SDHC / SDXC – Three Secure Digital card types with increasing maximum capacities (2 GB / 32 GB / 2 TB)
  • SD Express – The latest SD standard combining the SD form factor with PCIe and NVMe for speeds up to 985 MBps
