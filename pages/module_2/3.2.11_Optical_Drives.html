3.2.11 Optical Drives

What are Optical Drives?

Optical drives use a laser to read data encoded on the surface of discs such as CDs, DVDs, and Blu-ray Discs (BDs). While marketed as durable, scratches can make discs unreadable.

Disc Recording Formats

Format Write Sessions Can Erase?
Basic Recordable Written once in a single session No
Multisession Recordable Written across multiple sessions No
Rewritable Written multiple times (up to a write cycle limit) Yes

Capacity and Transfer Rates by Disc Type

Type Capacity Formats Base Transfer Rate
CD Up to 700 MB CD-R, CD-RW 150 KBps
DVD 4.7 GB (single-layer) to ~17 GB (dual-layer, double-sided) DVD+R/RW and DVD-R/RW (indicated by ± symbol) 1.32 MBps (= 9x CD speed)
Blu-ray 25 GB per layer BD-ROM (read-only), BD-RE (rewritable) 4.5 MBps base; up to 72 MBps (16x)

Installation and Connectivity

Type Bay / Connection Notes
Internal 5.25-inch drive bay; SATA data and power connectors Standard for desktop PCs
External USB, eSATA, or Thunderbolt Usually requires an AC adapter for power; tray-based or slot-loading mechanism

Other Features and Considerations

  • Speed rating – Expressed as record/rewrite/read (e.g. 24x/16x/52x); newer drives are typically multi-format
  • Emergency eject – A small hole on the drive face; insert a paper clip to manually eject the disc if the button fails or there is no power
  • DRM (Digital Rights Management) – Copy protection used on consumer DVDs and Blu-rays
  • Region coding – Restricts disc playback to players from the same geographic region; can be changed via device properties on a PC, but the firmware limits the number of changes allowed
  • Older drives may not support Blu-ray — check compatibility before purchasing

Key Terms

  • CD – Compact Disc; up to 700 MB; formats include CD-R and CD-RW
  • DVD – Digital Versatile Disc; up to ~17 GB; formats include DVD±R and DVD±RW
  • Blu-ray (BD) – High-capacity optical disc; 25 GB per layer; formats include BD-ROM and BD-RE
  • CD-R / DVD-R / BD-R – Recordable (write once) disc formats
  • CD-RW / DVD-RW / BD-RE – Rewritable disc formats (can be erased and rewritten)
  • DRM – Digital Rights Management; copy protection technology on commercial discs
  • Region coding – Locks disc playback to a specific geographic region
