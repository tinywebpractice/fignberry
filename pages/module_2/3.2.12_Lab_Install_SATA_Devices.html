Lab Scenario

A desktop computer needs additional SATA storage installed and connected correctly. Your task is to place the SATA devices in the proper bays, attach the SATA data cables to the motherboard, connect SATA power leads from the power supply, and confirm the system is ready to detect the new drives.

Step 1 - Identify the Required Devices

  • Review the lab instructions and determine which SATA devices must be installed, such as a hard drive, SSD, or optical drive.
  • Check that the case has the correct drive bays available for each device size.
  • Locate open SATA ports on the motherboard and available SATA power connectors from the PSU.

Step 2 - Mount the SATA Devices

  • Install each 3.5-inch or 2.5-inch storage drive into the correct internal drive bay.
  • If the lab includes an optical drive, slide it into the front-facing 5.25-inch bay.
  • Secure each device according to the case design so it does not shift after cabling.

Step 3 - Connect SATA Data Cables

  • Attach one end of a SATA data cable to each device.
  • Connect the other end of each cable to an available SATA port on the motherboard.
  • Keep the cable orientation correct. SATA connectors are keyed and should not be forced.

Note: A SATA device needs both a data connection to the motherboard and a power connection from the PSU.

Step 4 - Connect SATA Power

  • Find the 15-pin SATA power connectors coming from the power supply.
  • Connect one SATA power lead to each installed SATA device.
  • Verify that every drive has both cables attached firmly before moving on.

Step 5 - Verify the Installation

  • Check that no data or power cable is loose, twisted sharply, or blocking other components.
  • Confirm that each SATA data cable runs from the drive directly to a motherboard SATA port.
  • Power on the system or complete the lab validation step to verify that the devices are recognized.

Connection Summary

Component Connection Needed Destination
SATA hard drive or SSD SATA data cable Motherboard SATA port
SATA hard drive or SSD SATA power connector Power supply SATA lead
Optical drive SATA data cable Motherboard SATA port
Optical drive SATA power connector Power supply SATA lead

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Connecting only SATA power and forgetting the SATA data cable
  • Connecting the data cable to the drive but not to the motherboard
  • Using excessive force on keyed SATA connectors
  • Leaving a drive unsecured in the case before cabling is finished

Key Terms

  • SATA data cable - Cable that carries data between a SATA device and the motherboard
  • SATA power connector - 15-pin power connector from the PSU used by SATA devices
  • SATA port - Motherboard interface used to connect SATA storage and optical devices
  • Drive bay - Internal mounting location for a storage or optical device
  • Optical drive - CD, DVD, or Blu-ray drive that uses SATA for data and power in most desktop systems

Important: Before installing or cabling drives, make sure the system is powered off and unplugged.

CompTIA Lab Instructions

Complete this lab as follows:

  1. Identify the port locations for SATA connectors on the motherboard.
    • Above the computer, select Motherboard to switch to the motherboard view.
    • Select the Motherboard.
    • From the Selected Item pane, select the Item Details icon.
    • Review the Item Details to identify the location of the SATA ports.
    • Close the Item Details window after you have found the SATA ports.
  2. Install the SATA hard drives.
    • Switch the computer to the Drive Bays view.
    • Under Inventory, expand Storage.
    • Drag each hard drive from the Inventory to an open 3.5" drive bay.
  3. Attach the SATA cables to the hard drives.
    • Under Inventory, expand Cables.
    • Select a red SATA cable.
    • From the Selected Item pane, drag a SATA connector to a hard drive.
    • Under Inventory, select the other red SATA cable.
    • From the Selected Item pane, drag a SATA connector to the second hard drive.
  4. Attach the SATA cables to the motherboard.
    • Switch the computer to the Motherboard view.
    • Select the Partial Connections button, then select a SATA cable.
    • From the Selected Item pane, drag the unconnected SATA connector to a SATA port on the motherboard.
    • Repeat the previous two steps for the other cable.
  5. Connect power to both hard drives.
    • Switch to the Drive Bays view of the computer.
    • Above the computer, select Partial Connections, then select the power supply.
    • From the Selected Item pane, drag a SATA power connector to one of the hard drives.
    • Drag a different SATA power connector to the second hard drive.

Accessible Steps to Complete This Lab

  1. Add the two hard drives to the computer.
    • Press ALT+I to access the Inventory menu.
    • Tab to the Storage option and press Enter.
    • Tab to the first hard drive and press Enter. Press Enter again to add the drive.
    • In the Select Location to Add Item window, in the Connector End to Connect drop-down menu, press the down arrow key until Hard Drive, Internal, SATA is selected.
    • Tab to the Destination drop-down menu.
    • Press the down arrow key until 3 1/2 Inch Internal Bay 1 Standard ATX Case, v2015 is selected.
    • Tab twice and press Enter to confirm the selections.
    • Press ALT+I to access the Inventory menu.
    • Tab to the remaining hard drive and press Enter. Press Enter again to add the drive.
    • In the Select Location to Add Item window, in the Connector End to Connect drop-down menu, press the down arrow key until Hard Drive, Internal, SATA is selected.
    • Tab to the Destination drop-down menu.
    • Press the down arrow key until 3 1/2 Inch Internal Bay 2 Standard ATX Case, v2015 is selected.
    • Tab twice and press Enter to confirm the selections.
  2. Connect the hard drives to the motherboard.
    • Press ALT+I to access the Inventory menu.
    • Tab to the Cables option and press Enter.
    • Tab to the first SATA cable (red one) and press Enter. Press Enter again to add the cable.
    • In the Select Location to Add Item window, in the Connector End to Connect drop-down menu, press the down arrow key until Connector, SATA, 7-pin is selected.
    • Tab to the Destination drop-down menu.
    • Press the down arrow key until SATA1 Motherboard, Socket AM3, NIC is selected.
    • Tab twice and press Enter to confirm the selections.
    • Press ALT+I to access the Inventory menu.
    • Tab to the remaining SATA cable (red one) and press Enter. Press Enter again to add the cable.
    • In the Select Location to Add Item window, in the Connector End to Connect drop-down menu, press the down arrow key until Connector, SATA, 7-pin is selected.
    • Tab to the Destination drop-down menu.
    • Press the down arrow key until SATA2 Motherboard, Socket AM3, NIC is selected.
    • Tab twice and press Enter to confirm the selections.
    • Press ALT+W to access the Workspace.
    • Tab until the arrow next to System Case (ATX) is highlighted and press Enter.
    • Tab until the arrow next to Motherboard, Socket AM3 is highlighted and press Enter.
    • Tab until the arrow next to Connector, SATA, 7-pin is highlighted and press Enter.
    • Tab until the disconnected Connector, SATA, 7-pin (data) with a red icon is highlighted and press Enter. Press Enter again to add the connector.
    • On the Select Location to Add Item window, in the drop-down menu under Destination, press the down arrow key until SATA Data Port Hard Drive, Internal, SATA is shown.
    • Press Tab twice and then Enter to confirm.
    • Press ALT+W to access the Workspace. The menus should all still be expanded.
    • Tab until the arrow next to the partially connected Connector, SATA, 7-pin with a yellow icon is highlighted and press Enter.
    • Tab until the disconnected Connector, SATA, 7-pin (data) with a red icon is highlighted and press Enter. Press Enter again to add the connector.
    • On the Select Location to Add Item window, in the drop-down menu under Destination, press the down arrow key until SATA Data Port Hard Drive, Internal, SATA is shown.
    • Press Tab twice and then Enter to confirm.
  3. Connect the power connections to the SATA hard drives.
    • Press ALT+W to access the Workspace.
    • Tab until the arrow next to System Case (ATX) is highlighted and press Enter.
    • Tab until the arrow next to Power Supply, ATX, 20+4 pin, PCIe is highlighted and press Enter.
    • Tab until a Connector, SATA, 15-pin (power) that is not connected and has a red icon is highlighted and press Enter. Press Enter again to add the connector.
    • On the Select Location to Add Item window, in the drop-down menu under Destination, press the down arrow key until SATA Power Port Hard Drive, Internal, SATA is shown.
    • Press Tab twice and then Enter to confirm.
    • Repeat this process for a second SATA connector cable.
