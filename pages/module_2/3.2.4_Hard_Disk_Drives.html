3.2.4 Hard Disk Drives

How an HDD Works

A hard disk drive (HDD) stores data on metal or glass platters coated with a magnetic substance. Platters are mounted on a spindle and spin at high speeds. Each platter has a read/write head on both sides, moved by an actuator mechanism.

HDD Internal Components

Component Function
Platters Metal or glass discs coated with magnetic material; where data is stored
Spindle The central axis that the platters are mounted on and spin around
Read/Write Heads One on each side of every platter; reads and writes data magnetically
Actuator Moves the read/write heads across the platters to locate data

Drive Geometry

Each platter surface is divided into circular tracks, which are further divided into sectors. Each sector holds 512 bytes. This low-level structure is known as drive geometry.

Performance – Spindle Speed (RPM)

Drive Type Spindle Speed Transfer Rate Access Time
High-performance 15,000 RPM Up to 180 MBps Below 3 ms
High-performance 10,000 RPM — Below 3 ms
Standard desktop 7,200 RPM ~110 MBps ~6 ms
Low-power / laptop 5,400 RPM Lower ~6 ms+

Note: Most HDDs use a SATA interface to connect to the motherboard.

Key Terms

  • HDD – Hard Disk Drive; stores data magnetically on spinning platters
  • Platter – The magnetic disc inside an HDD where data is stored
  • Spindle – The central axis the platters rotate around
  • Read/Write Head – Component that reads and writes data on each platter surface
  • Actuator – Moves the read/write heads to the correct position on the platter
  • Track – A circular path on a platter surface where data is stored
  • Sector – A subdivision of a track; each holds 512 bytes
  • Drive geometry – The low-level structure of tracks and sectors on a platter
  • RPM – Revolutions Per Minute; measures spindle speed and affects HDD performance
  • Access time – The total delay for the read/write head to locate and reach data (includes seek time)
  • Seek time – The time it takes the actuator to move the read/write head to the correct track
  • Internal transfer rate – The speed at which read/write operations are performed on the platters (MBps)
